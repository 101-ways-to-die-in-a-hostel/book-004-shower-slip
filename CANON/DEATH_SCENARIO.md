# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Traumatic brain injury from fall in shower, followed by blood loss
**Category:** Environmental Hazards
**Duration:** Approximately 45 minutes of gradual decline, death from combination of TBI and exsanguination
**Witnesses:** None during death; body discovered by another guest approximately 2 hours later

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Subdural hematoma from blunt force trauma to temporal region when skull strikes exposed pipe. Compounded by continued blood loss from scalp laceration. Alcohol intoxication prevents clotting and masks pain that might have prompted seeking help.

### The Setup
- Shower floor tiles removed months ago, never replaced (exposed concrete)
- No non-slip mat provided
- Exposed pipe from removed shower fixture at head height
- Poor lighting (one flickering bulb)
- Mold growth on floor creating additional slip hazard
- No grab bars
- Drain frequently clogs, water pools

### The Trigger
Brad, still significantly intoxicated (estimated BAC 0.18), enters shower barefoot. His impaired balance and the combination of soap residue, standing water, and mold on bare concrete cause his foot to slip. He reaches for support, finds none, and falls backward.

### The Process

1. Right foot slips on mold patch near drain
2. Brad's arms windmill, finding nothing to grab
3. He falls backward, rotation carrying his head toward wall
4. Temporal region of skull strikes exposed pipe at approximately 15 mph
5. Impact creates 4-inch laceration and fractures temporal bone
6. Brad loses consciousness briefly (concussion)
7. Regains partial consciousness, confused, assumes he's "just drunk"
8. Attempts to stand, fails, decides to "rest" on shower floor
9. Blood loss continues from scalp wound - scalp wounds bleed profusely
10. Intracranial pressure builds from subdural hematoma
11. Consciousness fades over approximately 40 minutes
12. Cardiac arrest follows brain herniation

## Research Notes

- Bathroom falls are leading cause of accidental injury in domestic settings
- Scalp lacerations can lose 1+ liters of blood due to high vascularity
- Alcohol prevents blood clotting and impairs judgment about injury severity
- "Talk and die" phenomenon: TBI patients can seem lucid then deteriorate
- Similar deaths documented in hostels globally

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Brad has been on a two-week bender. Last night was particularly heavy - absinthe, beer, shots, substances he didn't identify. He woke up still drunk, which is normal now. The room smells like his own sweat and vomit. He needs to shower before the noon "recovery session" at the hostel bar.

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
At 10:34 AM, Brad enters the bathroom. He doesn't notice the standing water or the green-black mold near the drain. He steps into the shower stall, turns on the water (cold - the hot takes minutes), and reaches for the soap on the built-in shelf. As he shifts his weight, his right foot slides on the mold.

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)

**Second 0-1:**
Brad feels his foot go. It's a familiar sensation - he's fallen drunk dozens of times. His arms shoot out reflexively. They find nothing. The wet concrete walls are too slick. Where a grab bar should be, there's only a rusted bracket.

**Second 1-2:**
His body rotates as he falls. He sees the ceiling, then the wall, then the pipe - an exposed copper pipe where a showerhead used to be, capped but protruding. He has time to think "oh sh--"

**Second 2-3:**
Impact. The pipe catches him just above the right ear. The sound is wet and wrong - not just a thud but a crack. Stars explode in his vision. Then nothing.

**Minutes 1-5:**
Brad comes to. He's on the shower floor. Water is hitting his chest. His head hurts, but his head always hurts. He's confused but that's not new either. He thinks: "I fell. I'm pissed. I should get up." He tries. He can't. His limbs feel like they belong to someone else. "Okay," he thinks, "I'll just rest for a minute."

**Minutes 5-20:**
Brad drifts. The water is cold and it should bother him but it doesn't. Blood is mixing with the water, swirling toward the clogged drain. He can't see it properly - his right eye isn't working right. He thinks about his mum. He should call her. He'll call her after his shower. After his recovery beers. Tomorrow for sure.

**Minutes 20-40:**
His thoughts become less coherent. He's not sure if he's in Prague or back home. The shower sounds like the ocean. The Gold Coast. His sister building sandcastles. He was happy then. Before the drinking. Before being Brad the Lad. He was just Brad. A kid. His mum calling him in for dinner. Coming, mum. Coming.

**Final Moment:**
Brad's brain, swelling against his skull, finally compresses his brainstem. His breathing becomes irregular, then stops. His heart, already stressed by years of alcohol abuse, gives out three minutes later. The water keeps running. Brad O'Connor, who always said he'd sleep when he's dead, gets his wish.

### Immediate Aftermath
At 12:41 PM, German backpacker Klaus enters the bathroom to find Brad unresponsive on the shower floor in a pool of diluted blood. He initially assumes Brad is "passed out drunk again" - it takes him a moment to notice the head wound. He screams. Staff are called. Ambulance arrives 11 minutes later, pronounces Brad dead on scene. The hostel doesn't close - the party continues that night, muted but ongoing.

## The Horror Element
The horror is the banality. Brad dies naked on a dirty shower floor, alone, because of a missing bathmat and an exposed pipe. The horror is that no one came looking for him - everyone assumed he was sleeping it off. The horror is that this kind of death happens all the time and we joke about it: "he died as he lived, drunk."

## The Comedy Element
The dark irony: Brad always said "sleep when I'm dead" and "I'll die having fun." He got both wishes. His last act on Earth was trying to clean himself up for more drinking. The party hostel kept partying - his death barely interrupted the noon beer pong tournament.

## Writing Notes

### Pacing the Death
- Build shows Brad's lifestyle in detail - normalize the dysfunction
- Fall itself is fast (3 seconds)
- Death is medium-slow (45 minutes of fading)
- Create false hope with partial consciousness

### POV During Death
- In Brad's head throughout
- His confusion mirrors reader's horror
- The shift from "I fell drunk" to "I'm dying" is gradual
- Final thoughts are regressive, childlike, heartbreaking

### Graphic Content Level
- 5/10: The horror is emotional not visual
- Show: the fall, the blood in water, the confusion
- Imply: the medical specifics, the final moments
